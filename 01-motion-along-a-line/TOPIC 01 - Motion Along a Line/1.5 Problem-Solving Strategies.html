1.5 Problem-Solving Strategies

Learning Goal

In this module you will:

  • Apply problem-solving steps and strategies to solve problems of one-dimensional motion.
  • Apply strategies to determine whether or not the result of a problem is reasonable, and if not, determine the cause.

Some people find solving physics problems challenging and they find it difficult translating the words into symbols that can be manipulated, calculated and graphed. But it's this translation from words to symbols that is the core of solving problems in physics.

To solve a problem more easily, it helps to break it down into a series of smaller steps. The following strategy consists of three steps: prepare, solve, and assess.

Prepare: Identify important elements of the problem and collect information.

Drawing a picture

The picture lets you model the problem and identify the important elements. As you add information to your picture, the outline of the solution will take shape. A picture could be a motion diagram or a graph.

Collecting information - Identify what you have and what you need
The problem's statement may give you some values of variables. Other information may be implied, or looked up in a formula sheet, or estimated or measured.

Preliminary calculation

Some calculations, such as unit conversions, are best done in advance.
Solve: Actually do the maths or reasoning necessary to arrive at the answer needed. But don't forget the Prepare step!
Assess: An important step. Once you have an answer, you should check to see whether it makes sense. Ask yourself these questions:
Does my solution answer the question that was asked? Does my answer have the correct units and number of significant figures? Does the value I computed make physical sense? Can I estimate what the answer should be to check my solution? Does my final solution make sense in the context of the material I am learning?
